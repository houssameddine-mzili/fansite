About Linkin Park

Linkin Park is an American rock band from Agoura Hills, California. Formed in 1996, the band rose to international fame with their debut album Hybrid Theory (2000), which was certified Diamond by the RIAA in 2005 and multi-Platinum in several other countries.

Their sound is a fusion of heavy alternative rock, hip-hop, and electronic elements and has been categorized as nu metal and alternative rock. The band's lyrics explore themes such as personal and existential despair, psychological conflict, and societal issues.

Linkin Park is among both the best-selling bands of the 21st century and the world's best-selling music artists, having sold over 100 million records worldwide. They have won two Grammy Awards, six American Music Awards, two Billboard Music Awards, four MTV Video Music Awards, 10 MTV Europe Music Awards and three World Music Awards.

Band Members

Mike Shinoda

vocals, rapping, rhythm guitar, keyboards, samples (1996–2017), synthesizer (2008–2017)
Mike Shinoda, born Michael Kenji Shinoda on February 11, 1977, in Agoura, California, is a multifaceted American musician, singer, rapper, songwriter, and record producer. He is widely recognized for co-founding the rock band Linkin Park in 1996, where he serves as the co-lead vocalist, rhythm guitarist, keyboardist, primary songwriter, and producer.
In addition to his pivotal role in Linkin Park, Shinoda also ventured into solo projects. He founded the rap outfit Fort Minor, showcasing his skills in hip-hop music and production​​. His work spans various genres, including pop/rock, electronic, rap, and stage/screen compositions.

Brad Delson

lead guitar (1996–2017), backing vocals (2006–2017)
Bradford Phillip Delson, known as Brad Delson, was born on December 1, 1977, in Los Angeles, California, USA. He is widely recognized as the lead guitarist and a founding member of the renowned rock band Linkin Park. Delson grew up in Agoura Hills, California, and attended Agoura High School with his friend and future bandmate, Mike Shinoda. After graduating high school in 1995, he formed a band called Xero with Shinoda, which eventually evolved into Linkin Park.
In addition to his music career, Delson has also ventured into acting and producing. He is known for his work in the films "Transformers" (2007), "The Expendables 3" (2014), and "Miami Vice" (2006). He has been married to Elisa Delson since September 16, 2003, and the couple has three children.

Rob Bourdon

drums, percussion (1996–2017), occasional backing vocals (2006–2017)
Robert Gregory Bourdon, known as Rob Bourdon, was born on January 20, 1979, in Calabasas, California. He is an American musician, best known as the drummer and a co-founding member of the American rock band Linkin Park. Bourdon's early exposure to music was through classical piano lessons, but he soon gravitated towards drums, influenced by his father, who was a drummer in a classic rock band.
Bourdon, influenced by Aerosmith drummer Joey Kramer, developed a deep passion for drumming. He eventually became one of the founding members of Linkin Park, a band that gained international acclaim with hit songs like “Numb”, “In the End”, and “One More Light”. His skill and contributions to the band have been a significant factor in Linkin Park's worldwide success.

Joe Hahn

turntables, samples, programming (1996–2017), backing vocals (2006–2017)
Joseph "Joe" Hahn, born on March 15, 1977, in Dallas, Texas, is an American musician, DJ, director, and visual artist, renowned as the DJ and creative director of the rock band Linkin Park. He is a second-generation Korean American, raised in Glendale, California, and a graduate of Herbert Hoover High School.
In Linkin Park, Hahn is responsible for the scratching, turntables, sampling, and programming across all seven of the band's albums. His contributions extend beyond music; he, along with bandmate Mike Shinoda, is credited for much of Linkin Park's album artwork. Hahn has also directed many of the band's music videos, showcasing his diverse artistic talents.

Chester Bennington

lead vocals (1999–2017; his death), occasional rhythm guitar (2006–2017)
Chester Bennington (1976-2017) was an American musician, best known as the lead vocalist for the rock band Linkin Park. His powerful and emotional style, blending rock, metal, and rap, helped propel the band to international fame. Linkin Park's debut album, "Hybrid Theory" (2000), was a massive success, becoming one of the best-selling albums of the 21st century.
Bennington's music often delved into personal struggles, touching on themes of depression, substance abuse, and trauma, resonating deeply with fans. He was praised for his unique vocal style, combining raw screaming with melodic singing.
Sadly, Bennington struggled with mental health issues and addiction for much of his life. His death in 2017 by suicide was a tragic loss, deeply felt across the music world and beyond. His legacy continues to influence and inspire musicians and fans alike.

Dave Farrell

bass (1996–1999, 2000–2017), backing vocals (2006–2017)
Dave "Phoenix" Farrell is an American musician and bassist, best known as a member of the rock band Linkin Park. Born on February 8, 1977, in Plymouth, Massachusetts, Farrell developed an interest in music at a young age. He attended UCLA where he met future bandmates Brad Delson and Mike Shinoda.
Known for his quiet and reserved nature, Farrell's bass playing is a key element of Linkin Park's sound, underpinning their blend of heavy rock, electronic elements, and hip-hop. He has contributed to all of the band's studio albums, and his musicianship has been a consistent presence throughout the band's evolution in style.
